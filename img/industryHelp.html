Definition

An industry is any group of enterprises, or productive units, who make the same commodity using the same technology. More than one industry can produce the same commodity.

Industries use 'inputs' which are other commodities, used to make their product. Labour is an input to most industries. You can control which inputs an industry needs when you fill out its details.

Definition

A social class is a group of people with a similar type of income or revenue, for example workers whose revenue comes from wages, or capitalists whose revenue derives from profit. You can specify where a class's revenue comes from.

Social Classes own, and consume, consumer goods, according to rules which you can control.

a social class can have mixed revenue, for example small landholders or shopkeepers. the participation ratio specifies what proportion of this class's revenue comes from wages.
